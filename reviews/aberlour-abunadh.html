Aberlour A'bunadh Review

Steve's Verdict

Top 2 for me. Every winter I go through several bottles.

Aaron's Verdict

Top 5 Malt. If you're going to own a bombastic sherry malt, this is the best I've had.

A'bunadh is gaelic for "of the origin". We've taken to amiably calling this malt "Origin". Aberlour's made a brilliant no-age-statement, sherry finished cask strength powershouse (60.4% alcohol in this batch).

The first thing you notice about this malt is the deep reddish brown colour. It's darker and redder than almost any malt on the market. The nose is dominated by all the sherry cask elements: molasses, sugar, and sweetness. The more subtle traces are still "big" flavours: dark fruits like black cherry.

At 60% alcohol, this is going to pack a punch, no matter what you do. Aberlour has jammed such strength of flavour in the remaining 40% that they manage to take over from the pure alcohol, and give the malt a life beyond the initial burn.

The most distinctive part of this malt is that the inherent flavours of the malt are not over-powered by the alcohol content. Cask strength malts are valued by enthusiasts because the malt is close to the pure level of the cask. For modern production bottles, the bottler has already diluted the malt with water to 40%, but not cask strength malts. They retain the original characteristic of the malt straight from the cask. The trade-off is that you usually cut the malt with water to your own taste. A seasoned scotch drinker can enjoy Origin uncut, although we do recommend you do so with care. It hits like a truck.

Adding water makes this malt dangerously good. If you add enough water to reduce the alcohol content down to normal levels, the drink becomes more tractable. Both the intense flavour and the burn are relieved, but in good proportion: it's a smoother drink where the sweet, big flavours still overwhelm any trace of alcohol.

The finish is long and warming. It is a perfect winter malt because of the warming. Any trace of burn disappears, and it leaves a sweet, warm bit of comfort that radiates down the throat. It's a brandy experience, leaving you with a glow that lasts a few minutes with each sip.

sherry, sweet, cask strength
