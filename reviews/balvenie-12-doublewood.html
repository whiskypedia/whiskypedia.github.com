Balvenie 12 Doublewood Review

Steve's Verdict

It’s a one trick pony with a pretty good trick. Sweet sherry for a cold night.

Aaron's Verdict

I walk past this malt all the time. It is good at what it does, but there are better malts in its weight class.

The Balvenie is one of the oldest single malt distilleries still producing from the Speyside. It resides in Dufftown, sharing the town with many other famous distilleries such as Glenfiddich. Balvenie malts are generally floral and very sweet, and with the Doublewood advertising its sherry-cask finish, this malt holds true to that reputation.

The nose is just a sherry-like sweetness. If you want to find something else, go ahead, but we can't find it. It’s just nicely sweet.

There are probably the traditional flowery and citrus flavours in there somewhere, but they’re overwhelmed by the sweetness. It’s smooth for a 12-year old, which is also a nice characteristic of many Speysides. Sherry cask finishes stay for a while, and this is no different. It’s long, sweet, and satisfying. It’s a nice warming malt for a cold evening.

Water takes all the molasses and sherry flavours out. It’s still sweet, but lighter, like honey. The finish isn’t quite as strong, but it’s still relatively long and enjoyable. But if you want a Speyside without all the sherry, you can buy a whole bottle of something else rather than diluting it out of this one.

This is a solid Speyside malt, with its flowery undertones, and sherry dominating the nose and flavour. The Balvenie shares this niche with Macallan, Aberlour, and other characteristic Speyside single malts.

medium, sweet, sherry
