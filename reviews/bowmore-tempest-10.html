Bowmore Tempest

Steve's Verdict

Bowmore promises the essential Islay peaty, salty character in a smooth, integrated malt. This delivers the character, but there are non-cask strength malts that do it better.

Aaron's Verdict

A dram worthy of the Bowmore brand and surprisingly smooth at 56%. So ... don't forget this is cask strength after you've already got a bottle of this limited edition malt.

Bowmore malts are some of the smoothest Islay malts going. They're known for combining a beautiful smoothness with almost shocking flavours of phenol, peat smoke, and saltiness. This malt is a cask strength, coming in at 56%, so expect a bit of rough ride.

The nose has less salt and sea air than you'd expect in a Bowmore. It's deceptively subtle.

This malt has warming, smooth malty tastes that turn to smoky, peaty saltiness when you swallow. It's wonderfully integrated. The finish is long and all peat smoke. It's slightly less salty than some regular production Bowmores. But it's also more warming, and coats your throat like a cask strength malt should.

Water reduces the initial burst of flavour, but keeps the essential character. The finish is still long and peaty. The flavours are still relatively complex. Water it down if the cask strength alcohol percentage is too bitey, but this malt is very drinkable straight up.

Bowmore consistently bottles good experiences. They're a cornerstone of the Islay experience. They're integrated and smooth, all while putting some hair on your chest. This is no different, and will reward and malt enthusiast with a taste for Islay and cask strength malts.
