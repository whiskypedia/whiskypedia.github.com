Glenrothes 1994 Review

Steve's Verdict

Not the smoothest 12 year old, but rewarding if you like a little complexity.

Aaron's Verdict

Glenrothes is one of my favourite distilleries but there are better expressions of this distillery. Move along kids, nothing to see here.

The Glenrothes malts are generally admired by experienced scotch drinkers. The distillery puts out solid bottlings every few years, following an unusual pattern of listing the year the malt was made, rather than the number of years it was aged. This particular malt was made in 1994, but bottled in 2006, making it a twelve year old vintage.

The nose has malt, vanilla and a little butterscotch in the background. It’s well balanced — no one overshadows the rest.

First sip, there is more than a little burning. But once you soldier past, you’ll find floral notes intermixed with a vanilla and chocolate. The finish is mid to long with a little ginger-like burn to remind you of that first sip.

It’s a complex malt. We try to name the really dominant flavour, but some malts are just well integrated amongst their flavours. Bowmore is a good example from Islay, and Glenrothes is a nicely well balanced Speyside malt.

Glenrothes will always have a 12-year old out, even if it’s not this particular 12 year old. As a rule, they’re a treat to an experienced drinker who wants something more complex in the Speyside flavour than a Glenfiddich.

medium, complex
