Glen Arran Port Finish Review

Steve's Verdict

Excellently made, but gimmicky. I'd rather have the excellent Glen Arran 10.

This is an interesting malt to try because there aren't a lot of port cask finished single malts out there. It's part of Glen Arran's line of finishes with no age statement and among them the port finish has to be the oddest.

First, it's a pinky salmon colour. You don't realize how much it's taken for granted that a single malt should be some shade of orange, until it's not. I looked the bottle over for damage or an expiry date to make sure I got the real thing.

Colour aside, this malt packs a punch. It's semi-cask strength at 50%, but not too harsh to drink straight up. And the port finish adds enough flavour to keep up with the alcohol content.

But what about that finish? Port sweetens the malt like sherry, but it doesn't taste like sherry. The port finish has a more tart, grapey flavour to it. I can promise there's no finish like it.

It's an acquired taste. Sherried malts are a dime a dozen, and the more sherried, the less distinctive they are. But this port finish is unique and interesting, if not very familiar. Unfortunately, it's unique and interesting because of the finish, rather than the malt itself. Like sherry, the port takes over the malt, and it becomes all about the finish.

And there's a real shame in that. Glen Arran produces some of the best tasting young malts sold, all without having to drown them in the flavour of some other spirit. The port finish is good because Glen Arran seems incapable of releasing a poor malt, but it's still a gimmick.

That's not to say it's not worth buying. If you're interested in odd finishes slightly off the beaten path, you will enjoy this one.

port, sweet, finished
