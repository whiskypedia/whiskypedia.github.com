Auchentoshan 18 Review

Steve's Verdict

Too much burning for the price. For the same type of thing, buy the 10 year old and save the cash.

Aaron's Verdict

Underwhelming. What was that Malt doing for those 18 years? Certainly not maturing.

Auchentoshan is the quintessential lowland malt. The 10 year old has graced the LCBO shelves regularly for years. Unpeated lowlands are generally more delicate than your chest-hair growing Islay malts, so Auchentoshan has been a fantastic gift for ages, especially as an introductory single malt for blend drinkers.

The nose is weak. You have to stick your nose way down into the glass, and then all it offers up is your typical lowland fare: grassy citrus. It gets richer once you’ve had a sip. There’s nothing really distinctive here that you won’t find in another bottling of Auchentoshan, and probably for cheaper.

It's also spicier up front than your typical 18 year old. Relatively tame after half a glass, but still surprising. While starting relatively oily and warming, the blast of “spice” (i.e., burning) dominates the otherwise subtle sweet lemongrass flavour. Water is mandatory to bring out the more delicate flavour. It pushes any burning off to late into the finish, which means you enjoy each sip for longer.

The finish has a splash of the salty sea air that you get in larger quantities in an Islay malt like a Bowmore. It’s subtle, but there’s so little going on otherwise that it’s notable. Bowmore owns Auchentoshan, so while they’re hundreds of miles apart, and made in completely separate ways, it’s interesting that they have some tiny thread in common.

burn, unpeated
