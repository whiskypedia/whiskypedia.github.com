Aberlour 10 Review

Steve's Verdict

A solid choice on a budget for the Speyside taste, but without some of the sophisticated flavours of some older malts.

Aaron's Verdict

This is the kind of malt you wish your retailer told you about. An uncomplicated but enjoyable malt at a great price.

If you're looking for a straightforward Speyside malt with strong expressions of vanilla and sherry, this is a great choice with a great price tag. It's not sophisticated like other Speysides, but it's warm and comfortable.

The nose is sweet like bourbon, but not a sherry bomb. It has a medium body, but medium in a good way. Sweet without being too sweet. Long lasting but not oily. Medium in the way of being balanced.

I'm not a fan of saying that there are "hints of" anything, since that's usually code for a tasting snob. Like: "hints of tulip petals" or "hints of Paris in the spring". But this malt has hints of vanilla. Not an outright flavour, more like a reminder of vanilla. This is common in bourbon casked malts, and this particular malt is a good expression of it.

The dominant flavours are vanilla, sherry and a mild spiciness. Without water, the sherry gets in the way of the more subtle flavours that you find in Speyside malt. The delicate floral flavours might be in there, but it's too hidden with the bitey character of any 10-year old and the sweetness.

Water takes most of the sweet out of it, but leaves the bare malt. It isn't replaced with flowers that you'd expect from a more sophisticated Speyside. This malt doesn't need any water, and it doesn't help. Drink this straight up and enjoy it for what it is.

medium, sweet
