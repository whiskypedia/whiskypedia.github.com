Glenlivet 12 Review

Steve's Verdict

A little too boring to keep a bottle of this around, but I’m always happy to take a dram of the Glenlivet whenever offered.

Aaron's Verdict

Although the lack of rock and/or roll doesn’t make this one of my favourites, I always keep a bottle around for guests, my Dad and prospective single malt converts.

For many people, the Glenlivet is often a bridge between blended malt whiskey and single malts. Its smooth, consistent and subtle flavours make this a great introduction to single malt whiskey. This has made Glenlivet one of the most recognized single malt names in the world.

The nose on the 12 is sweet, but lighter and more delicate than a Bourbon’s sweetness. It’s not sugary like molasses. Speyside malts like Glenlivet are known for their subtle flavours and aromatic, almost flowery nose.

The Glienlivet’s primary flavour is a slightly sweet vanilla. It starts very smooth with almost nothing but vanilla, and then builds. A bit of bite to it, but certainly no more than any other 12 year old. The finish is short, and has a characteristic maltiness. It finishes more like a dry wine than a brandy. Water takes out any bite, but leaves the character of the malt untouched. It still is primarily vanilla and malty smoothness, just moreso. Delicate malts are usually erased by water, losing all distinctiveness. While the nose is wiped out, the Glenlivet’s trademark vanilla and smooth maltiness come into their own with a drop or two.

Many beginners start with the Glenlivet, and for good reason. Beginners should take note: this is a good, gentle introduction to the pleasure of single malts. It can remain a favourite, even when moving on to more distinctive malts.

light, vanilla
